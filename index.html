Scientific Abstract

Problem. Digital attribution — linking digital artifacts to their human source — underpins cyber threat intelligence, digital forensics, and counter-fraud. Current methods rely on technical identifiers that sophisticated adversaries can spoof or eliminate.

Research Gap. Behavioral attribution methods exist across stylometry, biometrics, and forensic linguistics but lack a unifying theoretical foundation, pre-specified validation criteria, and transparent documentation of limitations.

Methodology. The AnubisX framework comprises: (a) 16 axioms governing attribution reasoning; (b) Cognitive Centroid theory; (c) 292 mathematical objects across 24 categories; (d) 37 algorithms spanning five behavioral modalities; (e) four-tier validation infrastructure with 31 acceptance criteria.
